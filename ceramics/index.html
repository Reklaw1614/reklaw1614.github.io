Ceramics


Reptilian Humanoid

Some of my favorite classes in high school have been my ceramics classes, and one of my favorite assignments was to make a head-and-shoulder bust. I chose to make a sculpture of a reptilian humanoid. Before I started, I imagined what the sculpture would look like and sketched it out. Next, I formed the shape of a human head and dried it to the right texture and moisture content to allow for hollowing out the inside without sacrificing structural integrity. I worked in two parts - one was the head, and the other the bust. The bust was made from coils rather than a solid block to minimize weight and waste of material. When it came time to attach the two pieces, I used a scoring tool and slip for adherence. I cut and scored each scale individually (and there were hundreds).
