Robotic Ball Balancer

In my robotics class at UW-Madison, I designed, built, and programmed a joystick-operated robot that controls the position of a ping-pong ball by adjusting the angle of a servo-actuated track. It utilizes computer vision to track the position of the ball and a PID loop to bring the ball to the desired position. The robot has an LED strip that indicates the desired position and error.

Combat Robot Project

As a personal project, I made a small (sub 1.5 pound) combat robot. I designed and fabricated a waterjet-cut hardened steel blade driven by a brushless motor, designed and 3D-printed the chassis, modeled the assembly, sourced the components, programmed the controller, and executed the idea.
